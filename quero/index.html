How I work

The process is often not that linear, but the structure is as follows.

1) Immersion

When a task comes along, I try to talk with people involved and understand everything they know about that problem.

  • Conversation with PO/PM
  • Also talk with people from other departments, like customer service, operations and tech to get other points-of-view about that issue
  • Read any existing research or material about the topic
  • Do my own research

2) Hypothesis

After speaking with some people and researching, now I start to form my own opinions about how that should work.

  • Draw a few user journeys, so I can see the flow from start to end
  • Prototype something quickly (using pen and paper or via software) and show it to people involved to get the main idea right
  • Use their feedback to refine my ideas or come up with new ones

3) Prototyping and initial testing

When something seems promising, I start to draw and make it clickable to test and make sure it works.

  • Low fidelity prototype, using existing assets and design components
  • Testing the flow with product team and developers
  • Fix any inconsistencies or errors found during the tests
  • More tests with more people

4) Double down on design

At this point one or a few hypothesis should be more mature and it's time to refine them.

  • Work on UI to mid/high fidelity
  • Refine the interactions between the screens, like transitions
  • Test again with potential users and people not involved with the task
  • Refine and test again

5) Time to finish it

It's approved, tested and seems to work.

  • Final refinement on UI items like icons, shadows, colors, font-sizes and spacing
  • Handoff to developers
  • Release to a small group to beta-test before rolling out to production
  • Fix any problems found in this phase
  • Rollout to production
